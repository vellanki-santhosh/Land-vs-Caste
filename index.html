The Geography of Inequality

A Statistical Breakdown of Land Ownership by Social Community in India

Land has historically been the primary source of wealth, power, and social status in India. Based on aggregations of historical National Sample Survey Office (NSSO) and demographic data, a stark disparity exists between the demographic share of different communities and their corresponding share of land ownership. This infographic explores the distribution across General Categories (OC), Other Backward Classes (OBC), Scheduled Castes (SC), and Scheduled Tribes (ST).

Total Land Ownership Distribution

This visualization displays the estimated percentage of total agricultural and rural land owned by each major social group. Despite making up a smaller fraction of the population, the General Category (OC) controls a disproportionately massive segment of land, closely followed by OBCs.

Population Share vs. Land Share

Comparing population size to land ownership reveals the depth of structural inequity. While SCs comprise nearly 17% of the population, their land share is severely restricted to single digits. Conversely, the General Category's land ownership outpaces its population footprint.

Average Land Holding per Household

Beyond absolute totals, the quality and size of holdings matter. The average General Category household owns significantly larger tracts of land compared to marginalized communities. SC holdings are typically marginal and fragmented.

The Reality of Landlessness

A large percentage of rural households do not own agricultural land and are reliant on manual wage labor. The burden of landlessness disproportionately falls on historically marginalized communities.
